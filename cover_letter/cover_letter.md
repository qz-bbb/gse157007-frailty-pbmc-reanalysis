Dear Editors,

We submit "Donor-aware reanalysis of public single-cell PBMC data identifies constrained frailty-associated immune signals after platform sensitivity analysis" as an original research/data reanalysis article for consideration in Immunity & Ageing.

The manuscript presents a transparent reanalysis of GSE157007, a public human PBMC single-cell RNA and TCR dataset spanning cord blood, young adult, healthy-old/non-frail, and frail donors. The study emphasizes corrected donor labels, raw-data provenance, donor-aware statistics, and explicit platform/series sensitivity analysis.

The key finding is deliberately conservative: donor-level composition testing found no BH-significant frail versus healthy-old/non-frail differences, and an initial mixed-series Monocyte_CD14 pseudobulk signal did not survive F-series-only sensitivity. Thus, the manuscript provides a reproducible constraint on frailty-associated immune claims in this dataset rather than overstating a biomarker discovery. Descriptive TCR repertoire summaries are included as multimodal context.
